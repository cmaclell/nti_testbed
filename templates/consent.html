Study Description

This task is part of a research study being conducted by Soar Technology, Inc. The purpose of the research is to better understand how best to structure the interactions between a human and an Artificial Intelligence (AI) system when either the human or the AI is trying to teach the other something new.

Procedures

During this experiment, which we expect to take approximately 30 minutes, you will be asked to either teach an AI system to perform a range of robot manipulation tasks or the AI system will assume the role of the teacher and try and provide you with guidance on the same tasks. These interactions will take place through a web-based simulation environment and a web-based user interface.

Participant Requirements

Participation in this study is limited to individuals age 18 and older and fluent in English.

Additionally, all participants are required to have a modern web browser, such as Chrome, Safari, Edge, or Firefox (a requirement for using the web-based simulation).

Risks

The risks and discomfort associated with participation in this study are no greater than those ordinarily encountered in daily life or during other online activities.

Benefits

There may be no personal benefit from your participation in the study but the knowledge received may be of value to humanity.

Compensation and Costs

For participating in this task, you will be paid $2.625 directly through Mechanical Turk.

Additionally, there is an opportunity to receive a bonus on this task based on your performance as either a teacher or student. If you are randomly assigned the role of a teacher, then your bonus will be based on the performance of the AI system you teach on a pre-determined set of test tasks at the end of training (the test score will vary from 0 to 100 percent).

Similarly, if you're randomly assigned the role of a student, then your bonus will be based on your performance on a pre-determined set of test tasks after the AI system provides you with training.

A maximum bounus of $2 will be awarded for a perfect score on the test tasks and the bonus will be prorated for scores less than 100% (i.e., bonus = $2 * test score).

There will be no cost to you if you participate in this study.
